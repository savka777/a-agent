## Candidate Categories (for future use)

| Category | Why It's Good for Indies |
|----------|-------------------------|
| PDF & Document Tools | Boring = profitable. People pay to merge, compress, convert |
| Browser Extensions | Low barrier, good distribution, Chrome Web Store discovery |
| Meeting & Transcription Tools | Remote work boom, clear pain point |
| Link-in-Bio & Creator Tools | Creator economy, simple MVP, recurring revenue |
| Subscription & Expense Trackers | Everyone has too many subscriptions |
| Bookmark & Read-Later Tools | Niche but passionate users, premium pricing works |
| Resume & Portfolio Builders | Evergreen demand, clear conversion moments |
| Changelog & Roadmap Tools | Dev/startup audience pays, low competition |
| Waitlist & Launch Tools | Meta (tools for indie hackers), good Product Hunt fit |
| Flashcards & Spaced Repetition | Anki alternatives, students pay |
| Time Tracking & Timers | Freelancers need this, simple to build |

## Rejected Categories (and why)

| Category | Why Rejected |
|----------|-------------|
| Social & Dating | Network effects required, hard for indies |
| Travel | Dominated by big players, seasonal |
| Casual Games | Hit-driven, low margins, high volume needed |
| Finance & Crypto | Regulatory complexity, trust issues |
| Plant Care | Removed - market may be saturated after Planta success |

## Selection Criteria

Good indie categories have:
- Small enough market that big players ignore
- Solo dev can ship MVP in 2-4 weeks
- Active community on Product Hunt / Indie Hackers / Reddit
- Clear willingness to pay (not ad-supported)
- No network effects or massive content requirements
