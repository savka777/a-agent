# üß¨ Patterns Across Apps

**Pattern 1: The "Gold Rush" Shovel Strategy**
**Description:** A disproportionate number of winners (Queue, Senja, Screen Studio) are tools built *for* other builders/creators.
**The Play:** Don't try to be the next big consumer app. Build the boring infrastructure that the next big consumer app needs to run their business.

**Pattern 2: Automated Polish (The "Sleek" Effect)**
**Description:** Apps like Sleek and Screen Studio win because they remove the learning curve of "good taste." They don't just provide tools; they provide the *outcome*.
**The Play:** Stop building configuration settings. Build defaults that look amazing. Use AI to auto-format, auto-zoom, and auto-design.

**Pattern 3: Defensive AI**
**Description:** PayPerCrawl and OpenClaw signal a shift toward privacy and control. Users are tired of their data being vacuumed by big models.
**The Play:** Build "Local-First" alternatives to popular cloud tools, or build tools that block/monetize AI access to private data.

# üï≥Ô∏è Gaps in the Market

**Gap 1: "Screen Studio" for Mobile**
**Missing:** Screen Studio dominates desktop demos, but there is no equivalent "automated polish" tool for recording vertical mobile app demos that adds professional backgrounds, touches, and zooms automatically.

**Gap 2: Personal Data Monetization**
**Missing:** PayPerCrawl does this for websites. There is a gap for a "Personal PayPerCrawl"‚Äîa tool that helps individual creators or writers license their specific voice/data to AI models for a fee, rather than just being scraped for free.

**Gap 3: Vertical-Specific Infrastructure**
**Missing:** **Agentset** (launched Feb 2026) is building general AI infrastructure. There is a massive gap for *niche* AI infrastructure (e.g., "RAG infrastructure specifically for Legal firms" where privacy/compliance is the only feature that matters).
