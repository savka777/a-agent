# üß¨ Patterns Across Apps

Since we only have one app repeated 15 times, here's what the research *actually* reveals:

### Pattern 1: Distribution > Technology
Every single mention hammers this point. Grammarly's AI isn't magic - it's that they spent 15 years building keyboard integrations, browser extensions, and app partnerships. The lesson for indies: **build where users already are, not where you wish they were.**

**How to apply:** Don't build a standalone app. Build a plugin, extension, or integration for an existing platform with distribution (Notion, Obsidian, VS Code, etc.)

### Pattern 2: Freemium That Actually Works
Grammarly's free tier delivers real value (grammar/spelling). Premium unlocks tone, rewrites, and advanced features. This isn't a crippled trial - it's a genuinely useful product that creates word-of-mouth.

**How to apply:** Your free tier should solve a real problem completely, not just tease features. The upgrade should feel like "more of a good thing" not "finally usable."

### Pattern 3: Enterprise ‚â† Indie Opportunity
Grammarly makes serious money from B2B (50K+ organizations). But the research notes they have "enterprise SSO, brand voice training, governance" - features that are overkill for indie builders but table stakes for enterprise sales.

**How to apply:** If you're solo, don't chase enterprise. The sales cycle and feature requirements will kill you.
